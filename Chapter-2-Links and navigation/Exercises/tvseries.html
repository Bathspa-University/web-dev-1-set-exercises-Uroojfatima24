Stranger Things

Overview of the Plot

Stranger Things is set in the fictional rural town of Hawkins, Indiana, in the 1980s. The nearby Hawkins National Laboratory ostensibly performs scientific research for the United States Department of Energy but secretly experiments with the paranormal and supernatural, sometimes with human test subjects. They have inadvertently created a portal to an alternate dimension they refer to as the Upside Down, whose presence begins to affect the residents of Hawkins in calamitous ways. The first season begins in November 1983. Will Byers is abducted by a creature from the Upside Down. His mother, Joyce; the town's police chief, Jim Hopper; and a group of volunteers search for him. A young psychokinetic girl named Eleven escapes from the laboratory and is found by friends of Will. Eleven befriends and assists them in their efforts to find Will. The second season is set a year later in October 1984. Will has been rescued, but he begins having premonitions of the fall of Hawkins caused by a creature in the Upside Down. When it is discovered that Will is still being possessed by an entity from the Upside Down, his friends and family learn that there is a larger threat to their world. The third season is set several months later, in the days leading up to the Fourth of July celebration in 1985. The new Starcourt Mall has become the center of attention for Hawkins residents, putting the majority of other local stores out of business due to the mall's popularity. Hopper becomes increasingly concerned about Eleven and Mike's relationship and becomes very protective of his daughter. Unbeknownst to the town, a secret Soviet laboratory underneath Starcourt seeks to open the gateway to the Upside Down. Meanwhile, the Mind Flayer uses mind control to make Billy do his bidding. The fourth season is set several months later, in March 1986. Joyce, Will, Eleven, and Jonathan have moved to Lenora, California for a fresh start. In California, Eleven struggles with the loss of her powers and being bullied in school. Meanwhile, in Hawkins, a being from the Upside Down—an entity later dubbed Vecna—begins killing the residents of Hawkins, opening new gates between the two worlds in the process. Planning to stop Vecna, Dr. Sam Owens takes Eleven to a facility to help her regain her powers. Simultaneously, Joyce and Murray fly to Russia to rescue Hopper from the Gulag in Kamchatka.

Key Information

Development:

"Stranger Things" was created by the Duffer Brothers, who faced initial challenges pitching the concept to networks due to skepticism about child protagonists. After being approached by Dan Cohen and Shawn Levy of 21 Laps Entertainment, Netflix purchased the series for a full season. The setting was changed from Montauk to Hawkins, and the title "Stranger Things" was chosen. Visual and musical references from classic films of the 1970s and 1980s were used to establish the series' tone during the pitching process.


Writing:

The concept for "Stranger Things" stemmed from the Duffer Brothers' desire to expand the moral struggles of a father from the film "Prisoners" into a serialized television approach, focusing on a missing child and introducing a monster element grounded in Cold War-era science. They drew inspiration from various 1980s pop culture influences, including Stephen King novels, Spielberg films, and video games. Opting for an eight-episode format on Netflix allowed them to balance narrative development and characterization. They aimed to create a satisfying standalone season while leaving room for further exploration of the series' mythology. The characters, particularly the children, were influenced by the Duffer Brothers' own experiences as outcasts in high school.


Casting:

In June 2015, Winona Ryder and David Harbour joined the series "Stranger Things" as Joyce and the chief of police, respectively. Ryder was suggested for the role of Joyce by the casting director due to her prominence in 1980s films, while Harbour was chosen for his ability to portray a broken, flawed character. Additional casting included Finn Wolfhard, Millie Bobby Brown, Gaten Matarazzo, Caleb McLaughlin, Natalia Dyer, Charlie Heaton, and others. Auditions involved reading lines from "Stand by Me," with Wolfhard's film knowledge aiding his casting. Casting began before scripts were complete, allowing actors' interpretations to influence the characters. Matthew Modine provided input on his character, Dr. Brenner, helping to develop him further.


Filming:

The Duffer Brothers initially intended to film "Stranger Things" in Long Island but shifted to Atlanta, Georgia, due to weather constraints. Many locations in Georgia, including Atlanta and surrounding areas, served as the backdrop for filming, with Emory University, Bellwood Quarry, and Stone Mountain Park among them. After considering making the Starcourt Mall set a permanent attraction, producers ultimately decided against it. Production for the fourth season began in early 2020 but was temporarily halted due to the COVID-19 pandemic, later resuming in September 2020. Plans for the fifth and final season were delayed due to the 2023 Writers Guild of America strike.


Visual Effects:

The Duffer Brothers created an aged effect for "Stranger Things" by adding film grain over the footage, capturing the feel of 1980s films from Amblin Entertainment. They aimed to evoke mood and atmosphere over violence, aligning with the tone of PG-13-rated movies from that era. Practical effects were prioritized, including an animatronic Demogorgon, although digital effects were used for complex scenes. The title sequence, designed by Imaginary Forces, used sliding letters against a black background, inspired by 1980s title sequences and input from industry veterans. The sequence, initially developed with various fonts, was finalized with the ITC Benguiat font and incorporated computer-generated effects reminiscent of 1980s techniques.
